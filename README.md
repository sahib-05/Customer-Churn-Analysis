# Customer Churn Analysis

##  Overview

This project focuses on analyzing customer churn patterns using SQL/Python-based data analysis techniques. The main objective is to identify the key factors influencing customer churn and provide business recommendations to improve customer retention.

The analysis primarily examines contract types, payment methods, tenure, internet services, and customer demographics to understand why customers leave and how churn can be reduced. Customers on month-to-month contracts, electronic check payment methods, and shorter tenure periods showed the highest churn tendencies .

##  Key Findings

### Contract Type

Customers with month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate at nearly 42%, while one-year contracts show around 11% churn and two-year contracts only 3% .

### Payment Methods

Customers using electronic checks show the highest churn rate at 45%, while credit card and bank transfer users show much lower churn rates of around 15–18% .

### Customer Tenure

Customers with less than one year of tenure show nearly 50% churn, while customers staying for more than three years show only around 15% churn .

### Internet Service

Fiber optic users show higher churn compared to DSL users, indicating possible dissatisfaction or strong market competition .

### Senior Citizens

Senior citizens have a significantly higher churn rate (41%) compared to non-senior customers (26%) .

---

##  Business Recommendations

* Promote longer contract plans with attractive offers
* Encourage customers to shift from electronic checks to safer payment methods
* Improve onboarding and customer engagement during the first year
* Provide special retention strategies for senior citizens
* Improve service quality and support for fiber optic users

---

##  Conclusion

This analysis helps businesses understand the major reasons behind customer churn and provides actionable strategies to improve customer retention, customer satisfaction, and long-term revenue growth.
